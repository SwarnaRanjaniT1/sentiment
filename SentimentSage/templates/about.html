About the Sentiment Analyzer

Understanding how our sentiment analysis works and the technology behind it.

How It Works

Sentiment Analysis

Our sentiment analysis tool uses Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques to determine the emotional tone behind movie and TV show reviews. The analysis classifies text as:

Positive

Score ≥ 0.05

Neutral

-0.05 < Score < 0.05

Negative

Score ≤ -0.05

We use NLTK's VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner) sentiment analyzer, which is specifically attuned to sentiments expressed in social media and works well with movie reviews.

FAQ

Our sentiment analyzer uses VADER, which is specifically designed for social media text and short reviews. It typically achieves 70-80% accuracy on movie reviews. However, it may struggle with sarcasm, complex sentences, or industry-specific terminology.

We include two pre-loaded datasets: 30 popular movie reviews and 30 TV show reviews. These datasets include both positive and negative reviews for a variety of titles to demonstrate the analyzer's capabilities.

Key terms are extracted using natural language processing techniques. We tokenize the text, remove stop words (common words like "the", "a", etc.), and then count the frequency of the remaining words to identify the most significant terms in the review.

Currently, results are stored in your browser session and will be available until you clear your browser data or click the "Clear Results" button. Future versions may include the ability to export results to CSV or PDF formats.
